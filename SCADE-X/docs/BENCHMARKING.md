# SCADE-X Benchmarking & Evaluation Framework

This document outlines the evaluation methodology, metric definitions, and experimental design used to scientifically benchmark the SCADE-X platform against its constituent subsystems (ASTRA and SCADE).

---

## 1. Evaluation Methodology

The benchmarking framework treats ASTRA, SCADE, and SCADE-X as competing classifiers over a shared set of simulated procurement cases. The ground truth (`is_ground_truth_anomaly`) is generated during the synthetic data generation phase, representing known fraudulent or broken processes.

**Objective:** Prove mathematically that the Hybrid Risk-Aware Fusion in SCADE-X outperforms both the isolated latent behavioral modeling of ASTRA and the rigid deterministic conformance checking of SCADE.

---

## 2. Metrics Definitions

### 2.1 Standard Classification Metrics
Generated by `metrics_engine.py`:
- **ROC-AUC**: Evaluates the model's ability to rank true anomalies higher than normal variance across all decision thresholds. This is the primary comparative metric.
- **PR-AUC (Average Precision)**: Crucial for highly imbalanced datasets (e.g., 99% normal, 1% fraud). Evaluates precision across recall levels.
- **F1-Score**: Harmonic mean of Precision and Recall at the operational threshold.
- **FPR / FNR**: False Positive and False Negative Rates. Operational deployments seek to minimize FPR (alert fatigue) while aggressively capping FNR (missed fraud).
- **Calibration (Brier Score)**: Measures whether a predicted risk score of 0.8 actually translates to an 80% probability of fraud.

### 2.2 Custom Resilience Metrics
- **Disruption Detection Quality**: ROC-AUC computed between the ground truth and the categorical `disruption_severity` scalar. Tests if the engine accurately maps fraud to the correct operational damage tier.
- **Recovery Prioritization Quality**: Average Precision computed against the `escalation_priority` SLA tier, ensuring urgent cases correlate strongly with true positive anomalies.

---

## 3. Experimental Setup

The orchestrator (`scadex_benchmark.py`) runs four automated pipelines:
1. **Model Comparison**: Direct head-to-head metric generation.
2. **Resilience Evaluation**: Computing business-centric metrics.
3. **Ablation Studies**: Quantifying the marginal utility of each sub-component.
4. **Robustness Analysis**: Measuring performance degradation under hostile data conditions.

---

## 4. Ablation Methodology

To prove that no subsystem is dead weight, `ablation_engine.py` systematically masks out core components and recalculates the fusion math.

**Experiments:**
1. `SCADE-X full`: The baseline standard.
2. `SCADE-X without ASTRA`: Disables continuous behavioral intelligence. (Tests reliance on structure).
3. `SCADE-X without SCADE`: Disables strict deterministic rules. (Tests reliance on latent models).
4. `SCADE-X without resilience`: Removes the vulnerability amplification scalar. (Tests the value of contextual business fragility).
5. `SCADE-X without graph layer`: Masks topological supplier risk. (Tests graph utility in fraud).

The drop in ROC-AUC between the full model and the ablated model equals the *marginal contribution* of that component.

---

## 5. Robustness Methodology

Operational data pipelines are noisy, incomplete, and adversarial. `robustness_engine.py` evaluates system resilience against pipeline failures:

1. **ASTRA Subsystem Offline**: Simulates a PyTorch OOM or GPU failure where ASTRA returns `NaN` for all cases. The fusion engine must fallback gracefully to SCADE.
2. **30% Sparse Process Logs**: Simulates data ingestion failures dropping process traces.
3. **Noisy Behavioral Signals (+20%)**: Simulates adversarial evasion or concept drift where ASTRA outputs are corrupted by Gaussian noise.

The engine tracks the percentage degradation of F1 and AUC across these failure modes.

## 7. Limitations & Interpretation Guidance

1. **Threshold Sensitivity**: The direct comparison relies on static thresholds (ASTRA=0.75, SCADE=0.125, SCADE-X=0.65). AUC avoids this, but F1 scores are highly sensitive to these operational cutoffs.
2. **Synthetic Data Bound**: If the underlying synthetic generation logic is biased, the benchmarking will overstate performance. True validation requires deployment against historical, real-world SIEM and ERP logs.
