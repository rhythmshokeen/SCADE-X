# SCADE-X Resilience Intelligence Engine

This document provides a rigorous mathematical and architectural specification of the SCADE-X Resilience Intelligence Layer. The engine bridges the gap between binary anomaly detection (is it fraud?) and systemic risk management (how will it impact the organization?).

---

## 1. System Purpose & Research Justification

Traditional anomaly detection engines output a single probability or classification. In enterprise supply chains, a single "fraud" flag is operationally insufficient. 

A $500 missing inventory step (anomaly) has a drastically different operational impact than a $5,000,000 duplicated payment executed by an unauthorized manager acting through a compromised supplier account (anomaly). The resilience layer translates ASTRA's behavioral signals and SCADE's conformance signals into **Operational Intelligence**: estimating how a failure will propagate, how hard it is to recover, and what priority SLA should govern the response.

### 1.1 Methodology
The engine operates on the `unified_case_intelligence.csv` generated by the SCADE-X Fusion layer. It relies on deterministic risk scalars mapped through bounded mathematical aggregations (linear weights and multiplicative amplifications).

---

## 2. Mathematical Formulations

Let $\mathcal{A}$ represent ASTRA outputs, where higher scores $\in [0, 1]$ indicate higher anomalies.
Let $\mathcal{S}$ represent SCADE outputs, where lower scores $\in [0, 1]$ indicate higher anomalies (or failures). 

To unify operations, we invert SCADE scores when dealing with risk magnitudes: 
$$R_{\text{scade}} = 1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{score}}$$

### 2.1 Operational Fragility ($F_{op}$)
Operational fragility represents how close a process instance is to structural failure.

$$F_{op} = 0.4 \cdot \mathcal{A}_{\text{risk}} + 0.6 \cdot (1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{composite}})$$

*Justification:* SCADE's structural deviations represent immediate systemic failures (e.g. broken sequences), hence weighted heavier. ASTRA's latent behavioral anomalies provide predictive context.

### 2.2 Disruption Severity ($S_{dis}$)
Maps continuous fragility and discrete policy violations into categorical intensity.
Let $V$ be the number of SCADE perspective dimensions falling below the $0.875$ threshold.

- **CRITICAL**: $F_{op} > 0.85$ or $V \ge 3$
- **HIGH**: $F_{op} > 0.65$ or $V = 2$
- **MEDIUM**: $F_{op} > 0.40$ or $V = 1$
- **LOW**: Otherwise

### 2.3 Supplier Dependency Risk ($D_{sup}$)
Combines topological centrality with financial impact.
Let $G$ be the Graph Centrality Risk (from ASTRA). Let $M$ be the Financial Drift Risk ($1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{amount}}$).

$$D_{sup} = \min(1.0, \, G \cdot (1.0 + M))$$

*Justification:* Centrality amplifies financial risk exponentially. A highly central supplier executing a high-value deviation threatens supply chain liquidity.

### 2.4 Risk Propagation ($P_{risk}$)
Estimates the likelihood that a failure in one case triggers downstream cascade failures.
Let $B$ be Behavioral Risk (from ASTRA). Let $C$ be Control Flow Risk ($1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{cf}$).

$$P_{risk} = \min(1.0, \, 0.5 \cdot D_{sup} + 0.3 \cdot C + 0.2 \cdot B)$$

*Justification:* Dependency drives cascade width. Broken process control flow drives cascade depth.

### 2.5 Systemic Vulnerability ($V_{sys}$)
Integrates process failure with SIEM security failure.
Let $Sec$ be Security Threat Risk ($1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{security}}$).

$$V_{sys} = \min(1.0, \, 0.4 \cdot F_{op} + 0.4 \cdot P_{risk} + 0.2 \cdot Sec)$$

### 2.6 Resilience Score ($Res$)
The final metric representing the case's ability to withstand the observed deviations.

$$Res = 1.0 - V_{sys}$$

## 3. Recovery Analysis

The engine estimates the human and operational cost to audit and recover the disrupted process.

### 3.1 Recovery Complexity ($C_{rec}$)
Driven by temporal entanglement and personnel boundaries.
Let $T$ be Temporal Delay Risk ($1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{time}}$).
Let $R$ be Resource/SOD Risk ($1.0 - \mathcal{S}_{\text{resource}}$).
Let $I$ be Statistical Outlier Risk (ASTRA Isolation Forest).

$$C_{rec} = \min(1.0, \, 0.4 \cdot R + 0.4 \cdot T + 0.2 \cdot I)$$

*Justification:* Segregation of Duties (SOD) violations require legal and HR auditing, spiking complexity. High temporal deviations indicate buried processes taking weeks, complicating retroactive audits.

### 3.2 Business Impact ($I_{biz}$)
Translates categorical severity into a continuous business damage scalar.
Base impact ($B_{imp}$): LOW=0.1, MED=0.4, HIGH=0.7, CRIT=1.0.

$$I_{biz} = \min(1.0, \, B_{imp} \cdot (1.0 + 0.5 \cdot P_{risk}))$$

### 3.3 Recovery Priority (SLA)
Dictates triage priority for forensic response teams.
$$U_{total} = 0.7 \cdot I_{biz} + 0.3 \cdot C_{rec}$$

- **P1_CRITICAL**: $U_{total} > 0.8$
- **P2_URGENT**: $U_{total} > 0.6$
- **P3_ELEVATED**: $U_{total} > 0.35$
- **P4_ROUTINE**: Otherwise

## 4. Explainability & JSON Payloads

Every resilience output is inherently decomposable. The engine outputs a `contributing_signals` JSON string outlining precisely how a resilience score dropped. Example:

```json
{
  "primary_driver": "Structural (SCADE)",
  "violated_count": 3,
  "fragility_contribution": 0.81,
  "propagation_contribution": 0.62,
  "vulnerability_contribution": 0.78
}
```

---

## 5. Limitations & Assumptions

1. **Linear Combinations**: The current formulas use linear weighted sums. While bounded to 1.0, they assume risk variables are independent, which may not always hold true (e.g., temporal delays often correlate directly with structural bypasses).
2. **Missing Feature Propagation**: If ASTRA graph features are missing for a case, $D_{sup}$ defaults to tracking financial magnitude alone.
3. **Threshold Sensitivity**: The thresholds mapping values to `P1`, `P2`, etc., are statically defined. In production, these should be dynamically learned against historical incident response loads.
